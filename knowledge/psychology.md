# Trading Psychology Rules

## The Small Account Mindset

With $1,100, your #1 job is **survival**. You are not here to get rich quick. You are here to:
1. Learn to trade without blowing up
2. Build consistency over weeks and months
3. Grow the account slowly through disciplined execution
4. Graduate to larger size only when you've earned it

A 50% drawdown ($550) requires a 100% return to recover. Protect your capital above all else.

---

## The 10 Commandments

### 1. Thou Shalt Not Revenge Trade
After a loss, the urge to "make it back" is the strongest and most destructive emotion in trading.

**Rule**: After a loss, wait minimum 15 minutes before the next trade. After 2 consecutive losses, wait 30 minutes. If you feel angry or frustrated, close the platform.

**Ask yourself**: "Am I taking this trade because I see a valid setup, or because I want to recover my loss?"

### 2. Thou Shalt Not Overtrade
More trades ≠ more money. With a small account, every trade costs you $0.84 in commissions and mental energy.

**Rule**: Maximum 4 trades per day. Once you hit 4, you're done regardless of how you feel.

**Remember**: Professional traders often take only 1-2 trades per day. Your edge comes from selectivity.

### 3. Thou Shalt Always Use a Stop
Every trade must have a pre-defined stop loss. No exceptions. No "mental stops." The stop goes in the platform the moment you enter.

**Why**: Without a stop, one bad trade can destroy your account. A 50-point move against you = $250 = 23% of your account.

### 4. Thou Shalt Not Move Thy Stop Further Away
"Just a little more room" is the lie your brain tells you before a catastrophic loss. If your stop gets hit, the trade was wrong. Accept it.

**Rule**: Stops only move in the direction of profit. Never further from entry.

### 5. Thou Shalt Not Average Down
Never add to a losing position. You have 1 contract. If it's losing, exit or let your stop handle it. Never, ever add another contract to "improve your average."

### 6. Thou Shalt Follow the Plan
You wrote a plan before the session. Follow it. If your plan says "short at PDH," don't suddenly go long because "it looks bullish."

**Rule**: Only take trades that match a strategy in your playbook. Improvised trades are gambling.

### 7. Thou Shalt Respect the Daily Limit
$55 daily loss limit is a HARD STOP. Not a suggestion. Not "one more try." When you hit it, the day is over.

**Why**: The market will be here tomorrow. Your capital might not be if you don't respect limits.

### 8. Thou Shalt Journal Every Trade
Write down every trade: entry, exit, stop, target, strategy, result, and what you were thinking. This is how you learn.

**Rule**: If you don't journal it, it didn't happen. Review your journal weekly.

### 9. Thou Shalt Accept Losses as Costs
Losses are not failures. They are the cost of doing business, like a store paying rent. Even the best traders lose 40-50% of their trades.

**What matters**: Are your winners bigger than your losers? Is your expectancy positive over 20+ trades?

### 10. Thou Shalt Not Compare
Don't compare your $1,100 account to someone trading $100,000. Don't compare your 1 MES contract to someone trading 10 ES contracts. Your only competition is your past self.

## Emotional States and Actions

### Recognizing Your State

| State | Signs | Action |
|-------|-------|--------|
| **Focused** | Calm, patient, following the plan, waiting for setups | ✅ Trade normally |
| **Excited** | Wanting to trade "any" setup, feeling lucky | ⚠️ Slow down. Only A+ setups. |
| **Frustrated** | Angry at a loss, wanting revenge, moving stops | 🛑 STOP. 30-min break minimum. |
| **Fearful** | Hesitating on valid setups, cutting winners too early | ⚠️ Reduce size to paper trade. |
| **Bored** | Taking trades just to "do something" | 🛑 Close the platform. Go for a walk. |
| **Euphoric** | Just had a big win, feeling invincible | ⚠️ Stop trading for the day. Lock in the gain. |
| **Exhausted** | Tired, distracted, can't focus on the chart | 🛑 STOP. Trading tired = trading drunk. |

### The 5-Second Reset
When you feel an urge to make an impulsive trade:
1. Take your hand off the mouse
2. Take 5 deep breaths
3. Ask: "Is this in my plan?"
4. Ask: "What's my risk?"
5. If both answers aren't clear → DON'T TRADE

## Weekly Mental Framework

### Monday
- Fresh start. Set weekly goals (process goals, not P&L goals).
- Example: "This week I will follow my stop rules on 100% of trades."

### Tuesday–Thursday
- Execution days. Focus on your plan, execute your strategies.
- Journal every trade same day.

### Friday
- Shorter trading day. Reduced conviction.
- Review the week's trades in the evening or weekend.
- Celebrate process wins, not just P&L wins.

### Weekend
- Review your journal. Look for patterns in your behavior, not just the market.
- Ask: "Did I follow my rules this week?" — this matters more than P&L.
- Prepare for next week: economic calendar, key levels, goals.

## Growth Milestones

Track these to measure real progress (not just P&L):

### Stage 1: Survival (Weeks 1-4)
- Goal: Don't blow the account
- Metric: Account drawdown < 20% ($220)
- Focus: Following rules, journaling, learning the market rhythm
- Success = still trading at the end of the month

### Stage 2: Consistency (Weeks 5-12)
- Goal: Break even or small profit after commissions
- Metric: Win rate > 40% with positive R:R
- Focus: Recognizing setups, managing trades, emotional control
- Success = 2 consecutive green weeks

### Stage 3: Growth (Months 3-6)
- Goal: Consistent weekly profitability
- Metric: Positive expectancy over 50+ trades
- Focus: Increasing average winner, reducing average loser
- Success = account growth to $1,500+

### Stage 4: Scale (Month 6+)
- Goal: Add a second contract
- Metric: Account at $2,100+ with sustained consistency
- Focus: Managing multiple contracts, partial exits
- Success = smooth transition to 2 contracts without behavior change

## The Hardest Truth

90% of retail traders lose money. The ones who survive and eventually profit share these traits:
1. They treat it like a business, not gambling
2. They focus on process over outcomes
3. They keep losses small and let winners run
4. They journal and review obsessively
5. They adapt when the market changes
6. They take breaks when they need to

You're starting with $1,100. This is your tuition. Pay it wisely.
