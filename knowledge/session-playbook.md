# Session Playbooks

## London AM Playbook (09:00–15:30 CET)

### Pre-Session Prep (08:30–09:00 CET)
1. Check the economic calendar — any European data releases? (ECB, PMI, etc.)
2. Note overnight range: ONH, ONL, overnight direction
3. Where is price relative to PDC? (bullish/bearish lean)
4. Mark key levels on chart: PDH, PDL, PDC, ONH, ONL, round numbers
5. Check US futures pre-market direction
6. Determine your bias for the morning

### Phase 1: London Open (09:00–09:30 CET)
**Character**: Initial volatility as European markets open. Often sets the morning direction.

**Approach**:
- DO NOT trade in the first 5 minutes. Let the noise settle.
- Watch the 09:00-09:15 range form (15-min Opening Range)
- If European data drops at 09:00: wait until 09:10 minimum

**Primary Strategy**: Opening Range Breakout
- Mark high/low of 09:00-09:15 candle
- Trade the break with stop on opposite side
- Target: 1.5-2× range width or next key level

**If no clear break by 09:30**: Shift to observation mode. The market is undecided.

### Phase 2: London Morning (09:30–12:00 CET)
**Character**: Follow-through from the open or range development.

**If trending from the open**:
- Strategy 3 (Trend Continuation): Wait for pullback, enter in trend direction
- Don't fade the trend — it often runs until US pre-market

**If ranging**:
- Strategy 2 (VWAP Mean Reversion): Fade deviations from VWAP
- Keep targets tight (VWAP to opposite side of range)

**Key times**:
- 10:00-10:30 CET: Second wave of European data sometimes released
- 11:00 CET: Often a reversal/acceleration point

### Phase 3: London Lunch (12:00–14:00 CET)
**Character**: Lower volume as European traders take lunch. Often the quietest part of your London session.

**Approach**:
- Reduce activity. Only take A+ setups.
- Good time to review morning trades and prep for NY overlap
- If you're up on the day, consider stopping here and coming back for NY
- If you're down, this is NOT the time to try to make it back — wait for NY volume

### Phase 4: Pre-US Open (14:00–15:30 CET)
**Character**: Volume increases as US pre-market traders wake up. Often the beginning of the day's biggest move.

**Key time**: 14:30 CET (8:30 AM ET) — Major US economic releases (CPI, NFP, GDP, Retail Sales, Jobless Claims)

**If economic data drops at 14:30**:
- DO NOT trade for 5 minutes after the release
- Let the spike settle, then assess the new direction
- Trade the reaction, not the initial spike

**Approach**:
- Mark the level where price was just before 14:30
- Strategy 4 (Level Reaction): Key prior day levels often get tested now
- Position for the US open direction if a clear trend develops

### London AM Summary
| Time (CET) | Phase | Primary Strategy | Intensity |
|------------|-------|-----------------|-----------|
| 08:30-09:00 | Prep | None — preparation | Low |
| 09:00-09:30 | Open | ORB | Medium-High |
| 09:30-12:00 | Morning | Trend/Mean Reversion | Medium |
| 12:00-14:00 | Lunch | Reduced activity | Low |
| 14:00-15:30 | Pre-US | Level Reaction | Medium-High |

**Max trades London AM**: 2 (save 2 for NY PM)

## NY PM Playbook (15:30–22:00 CET)

### Pre-Session Prep (15:00–15:30 CET)
1. Review London AM: What happened? Any open positions? (Should be flat.)
2. Update key levels: Has a new ONH/ONL formed during London?
3. Where is price relative to morning's range?
4. Check for any scheduled 16:00 CET (10:00 AM ET) data releases
5. Mark IB reference: The first hour (15:30-16:30) will define the IB
6. Update your bias based on London action

### Phase 1: US Cash Open (15:30–16:00 CET)
**Character**: Most volatile 30 minutes of the day. Institutional flow, opening rotations, gap fills.

**Critical Rules**:
- DO NOT chase the first candle. It often reverses.
- Wait for the first 5-min candle to close, then assess
- The "first 15-min fake-out" is extremely common — price spikes one way then reverses

**Primary Strategy**: Opening Range Breakout (30-min)
- Mark high/low of 15:30-16:00 range
- Wait for a candle to close beyond the range
- Stop on opposite side (or midpoint if range is wide)

**If gap open** (price far from PDC):
- Gaps in MES tend to get tested (not always filled)
- Don't trade the gap fill directly — wait for a level test
- If gap holds for 30 min, it's probably real — trade in gap direction

### Phase 2: Initial Balance (16:00–16:30 CET)
**Character**: Completing the first hour of RTH. Sets the day's reference range.

**After IB completes, assess**:
- IB width < 8 points → Expect expansion. Trade the IB breakout.
- IB width 8-15 points → Normal. Trade pullbacks within or breakout.
- IB width > 15 points → Range may be set. Fade extremes back to IB mid.

### Phase 3: NY Midday (16:30–19:00 CET)
**Character**: US lunchtime. Volume drops, price chops. The most dangerous period for overtrading.

**Approach**:
- REDUCE ACTIVITY or sit out entirely
- Only take trades if a clear trend from the open is continuing
- Don't start new positions — wait for the afternoon wave
- Good time to journal your morning/opening trades
- If you're at 3 trades for the day, consider stopping

**Exception**: If it's a strong trend day (price never looked back from open), this period still offers pullback entries. But these are rare.

### Phase 4: NY Afternoon (19:00–21:30 CET)
**Character**: Second wind of activity. Closing imbalances start. Often resolves the day's direction.

**Primary Strategy**: Trend Continuation or Trend Reversal
- If the trend from the open is intact → Strategy 3 (pullback entries)
- If the market has been ranging all day → Watch for a late breakout
- Last hour (21:00-22:00) brings MOC (Market on Close) flow — can create strong directional moves

**Key time**: 21:30-21:45 CET — MOC imbalance often published, creates last-minute directional push

### Phase 5: Wind Down (21:30–22:00 CET)
**Character**: Closing minutes of US RTH.

**Rules**:
- NO new positions after 21:30 CET
- If holding a position, look to exit by 21:45 at the latest
- DO NOT hold into the post-market / overnight
- Book your P&L and close the platform

### NY PM Summary
| Time (CET) | Phase | Primary Strategy | Intensity |
|------------|-------|-----------------|-----------|
| 15:00-15:30 | Prep | None — preparation | Low |
| 15:30-16:00 | US Open | ORB | **Very High** |
| 16:00-16:30 | IB | IB analysis | High |
| 16:30-19:00 | Midday | Reduced / sit out | **Low** |
| 19:00-21:30 | Afternoon | Trend/Reversal | Medium-High |
| 21:30-22:00 | Wind down | Exit only | Low |

**Max trades NY PM**: 2 (combined daily max: 4)

## Combining Sessions

### If You Trade Both Sessions in a Day
- **Morning P&L affects evening approach**:
  - Up $30+ in London → Trade NY conservatively. Protect your gains.
  - Flat in London → Full normal approach for NY.
  - Down $20+ in London → Reduce NY to 1 trade max. Don't chase losses.
  - Down $40+ in London → DO NOT TRADE NY. You're near your daily limit.

### Best Single Session to Trade
If you can only trade ONE session, choose **NY PM (15:30-22:00 CET)**:
- Highest volume and liquidity
- Clearest setups (opening range, IB breaks)
- Best follow-through on moves
- Most of your key levels get tested during this window
