# Market Structure & Key Levels

## Key Levels to Mark Before Each Session

### Tier 1 — Must Have (mark these EVERY day)
| Level | What It Is | Why It Matters |
|-------|-----------|----------------|
| **PDH** | Prior Day High | Upper boundary of yesterday's range. Break above = bullish, rejection = bearish |
| **PDL** | Prior Day Low | Lower boundary of yesterday's range. Break below = bearish, rejection = bullish |
| **PDC** | Prior Day Close | Settlement reference. Price above PDC = bullish bias, below = bearish bias |
| **ONH** | Overnight High | Highest price during 00:00-09:00 CET (or since prior US close) |
| **ONL** | Overnight Low | Lowest price during overnight session |

### Tier 2 — Important
| Level | What It Is | Why It Matters |
|-------|-----------|----------------|
| **VWAP** | Volume-Weighted Average Price | Institutional benchmark. Mean reversion anchor. Resets at RTH open (15:30 CET) |
| **IB High** | Initial Balance High | High of first hour of RTH (15:30-16:30 CET). Sets day's potential range |
| **IB Low** | Initial Balance Low | Low of first hour of RTH |
| **Round Numbers** | 5800, 5850, 5900, etc. | Psychological support/resistance. Options strikes cluster here |

### Tier 3 — Context
| Level | What It Is | Why It Matters |
|-------|-----------|----------------|
| **Weekly H/L** | Week's high and low | Broader range context |
| **Monthly H/L** | Month's high and low | Major support/resistance |
| **50-pt Round** | 5750, 5800, 5850 | Strongest psychological levels |
| **Prior VPOC** | Prior day's Volume Point of Control | Where most volume traded yesterday — strong magnet |

## How to Use Key Levels

### Price Relative to PDC
- **Above PDC**: Bullish bias for the day. Look for longs on pullbacks.
- **Below PDC**: Bearish bias for the day. Look for shorts on rallies.
- **Oscillating around PDC**: Indecisive. Wait for a clear break and hold.

### Overnight Range Context
- **Narrow overnight range** (< 10 points): Expect expansion during London/NY. Good for ORB strategy.
- **Wide overnight range** (> 25 points): Market may be tired. Expect mean reversion or continuation of overnight direction.
- **Gap** (open significantly above/below prior close): Gaps in futures often get tested but may not fully fill. Trade the retest of the gap edge.

### Initial Balance (IB) — First Hour of RTH
The IB (15:30-16:30 CET) sets the tone for the rest of the US session:

| IB Width | Implication | Strategy |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Narrow** (< 8 pts) | Expect range expansion. Breakout likely. | Trade the IB break |
| **Medium** (8-15 pts) | Normal. Could go either way. | Wait for direction, trade pullbacks |
| **Wide** (> 15 pts) | Major move already happened. | Fade extremes, mean revert to IB midpoint |

## Market Types

### How to Identify Early in the Session

**Trend Day** (~15-20% of days)
- Opens and moves directionally with few pullbacks
- Price stays on one side of VWAP all day
- Each pullback is shallow and quickly reversed
- Volume is strong and consistent
- **Action**: Hold winners, add on pullbacks, don't fade the trend

**Range Day** (~50-60% of days)
- Price oscillates in a defined range
- VWAP is flat and price crosses it multiple times
- Failed breakouts above/below the range
- **Action**: Fade the extremes, target VWAP, take quick profits

**Breakout Day** (~10-15% of days)
- Starts as a range day, then breaks out with conviction
- Often follows a catalyst (news, data release)
- High volume on the breakout candle
- **Action**: Trade the breakout, don't fade it, trail your stop

**Chop Day** (~10-15% of days)
- Random, directionless price action
- Tight range, lots of wicks, false breakouts
- No clear pattern on any timeframe
- **Action**: SIT OUT. This is the most dangerous market type for a small account.

### Early Warning Signs of a Chop Day
- Multiple failed breakouts in both directions in the first hour
- Candles with long wicks on both sides
- Volume declining despite price movement
- Price repeatedly crossing VWAP without follow-through
- **If you suspect chop: reduce your trade count or stop trading.**

## Support & Resistance Principles

### What Makes a Level Strong?
1. **Multiple touches** (tested 2+ times = confirmed)
2. **Confluence** (prior day level + round number + VWAP = very strong)
3. **Timeframe** (weekly levels > daily levels > intraday levels)
4. **Volume** (high volume at a price = strong level)
5. **Recency** (recent levels matter more than old ones)

### Level Interaction Rules
- **First touch**: Highest probability of reaction. Take the trade.
- **Second touch**: Still valid but reaction may be weaker. Tighten stop.
- **Third touch**: Level is weakening. Expect a break. Either skip or trade the break instead.
- **After a break**: The broken level becomes the opposite (resistance becomes support and vice versa). Wait for a retest to enter.

## Context Framework — Daily Bias

Before each session, determine your bias by answering:

1. **Where did we close yesterday?** (strong close near highs = bullish, near lows = bearish)
2. **What happened overnight?** (continuation or reversal of yesterday's move?)
3. **Where is price relative to PDH/PDL/PDC?** (above PDC = bullish lean)
4. **Any gaps?** (gap up = short-term bullish, gap fills are common)
5. **Economic calendar?** (major events create uncertainty — reduce bias conviction)
6. **Broader trend?** (what's the weekly/monthly direction?)

**Bias is a LEAN, not a commitment.** If the market proves your bias wrong, adapt. Don't fight the market.
