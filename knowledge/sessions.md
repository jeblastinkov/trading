## Your Trading Windows

### London AM Session (09:00–15:30 CET)
**Character**: European markets driving, building the day's range. Often sets the tone before US open.

- **09:00–09:30**: London open. European economic data releases (PMI, ECB, etc.). Initial directional move. Wait for the first 15-min candle to close before acting.
- **09:30–10:30**: Follow-through or reversal of the opening move. Good for Opening Range Breakout strategy.
- **10:30–14:00**: Range consolidation. Price often builds value. Best for VWAP mean reversion if price deviates from developing VWAP.
- **14:00–15:30**: Pre-US open positioning. Volume starts building. Overlap strategies kick in.

**Best setups**: Opening Range Breakout (09:00–09:30), VWAP Mean Reversion (10:30–14:00)

### NY PM Session (15:30–22:00 CET)
**Character**: US cash market open brings the most volume and strongest moves of the day.

- **15:30–16:00**: US cash open. Explosive volatility. Opening range forms. DO NOT chase the first candle — wait for confirmation.
- **16:00–16:30**: Initial Balance (IB) completes. Tells you if it's a trend or range day.
- **16:30–19:00**: US lunchtime. Often choppy, low-conviction moves. Reduce activity or sit out.
- **19:00–20:00**: Afternoon session picks up. Look for trend continuation or reversal.
- **20:00–22:00**: Final hour of US RTH. Closing imbalances. MOC (Market on Close) orders create directional moves in last 15 min. Good for trend completion trades.

**Best setups**: Opening Range Breakout (15:30–16:00), Trend Continuation (14:00–17:30 overlap), Prior Day Level Reaction (throughout)

### Key Time Markers (CET)
- **08:00**: European pre-market data sometimes released
- **09:00**: London open / European session starts
- **14:30**: US pre-market economic data (8:30 AM ET) — CPI, NFP, GDP, Retail Sales
- **15:30**: US cash market opens (9:30 AM ET) — highest volatility spike
- **16:30**: Initial Balance complete (first hour of RTH)
- **20:00**: FOMC announcements (2:00 PM ET) on meeting days
- **20:30**: FOMC press conference
- **22:00**: US cash market closes (4:00 PM ET)
- **23:00**: Daily halt begins

### Daylight Saving Time Warning
- EU switches: Last Sunday of March (to CEST) and last Sunday of October (back to CET)
- US switches: 2nd Sunday of March and 1st Sunday of November
- **Gap period** (2-3 weeks): US and EU are out of sync. During this time, all US event times shift by 1 hour relative to CET. Adjust your schedule accordingly.

## Day-of-Week Patterns

| Day | Typical Character |
|-----|-------------------|
| **Monday** | Often range-bound, finding direction after weekend. Lower volume. |
| **Tuesday** | Trend development. Often sets the week's direction. |
| **Wednesday** | FOMC days (when scheduled). Can be range-bound until announcement. |
| **Thursday** | Often continuation of Tuesday's trend. Jobless claims at 14:30 CET. |
| **Friday** | NFP first Friday of month. Often reversal or profit-taking day. Reduced afternoon volume. |
